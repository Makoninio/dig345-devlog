#### [Why everything is binary](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LbjxvvKEtyE)

##### Summary

The video explores how modern computing represents all content using binary (0s and 1s). Heydon Pickering proceeds to explain how binary thinking
is not just limited to modern computing systems and instead is evident in how humans think about the world. While binary is efficient for machines,
it heavily limits the human ability to go through the world because reality is too nuanced and complicated to be simplified to a series of binary decisions.
Using examples such as the human brain and the design of organisations, Pickering highlights how phenomena that should exist on a spectrum is often presented
as being conflicting.

##### Quote

Supposedly, everyone's personality is switched to one side or the other. There is only one minor problem with thi theory. Its bollocks

##### Comment

This made me think about how as someone hoping to design software interfaces in the future, how easy it is to fall into the trap of forcing users into rigid categories 
(yes/no, male/female, pass/fail) even when their real experiences are more complex. As someone building products and tools, this is a powerful reminder that technical 
simplicity should not come at the cost of accurately representing human diversity and edge cases.

##### Discussion Question

How can we balance the need for simplicity in bnary systems and the reality that human experiences often exist on a spectrum
